Testing
Tests to validate for the function Term.Subst.unify

unify at l unifies the pairs of term in l and returns the most general substitution that unifies them, i.e., \(mgu(l)\). When the subtitution \( \sigma \) obtained is of the form \(\sigma = \{ x \rightarrow y \}\sigma'\) for some variables \(x,y\) of same type (i.e., \(x,y \in \mathcal{X}^1 \) when the substitution is on protocol term, and \(x,y \in \mathcal{X}^2_{i}\) for some \(i\) when the substitution is on recipe) and some substitution \(\sigma'\), then the following properties hold:

  • \(x\) is existential implies \(y\) is either existential or free
  • \(x\) is free implies \(y\) is free
If no most general unifier exists then \(\bot\) will be displayed as result.

Note that we adopt the following convention:

  • Free first-order (resp. second-order variables) will be denoted by \(w\) (resp. \(W\) )
  • Existential first-order (resp. second-order variables) will be denoted by \(x\) or \(y\) (resp. \(X\) or \(Y\) )
  • Universal first-order (resp. second-order variables) will be denoted by \(z\) (resp. \(Z\) )
  • Public names will be denoted by \(a\) or \(b\) or \(c\)
  • Bound names will be denoted by \(k\) or \(l\) or \(m\)
